Since LaTeX markup is presented in a linear fashion, it demands a heavier cognitive cost than image display in human short-term memory (Clark & Paivio, 1991). As P4 mentioned, on the other hand, blind students often utilize refreshable braille display, the hardware device for presenting literary braille chords in a tactile form (Figure 5.1).

Figure 5.1: Email corpus representing blind students’ challenge in lectures.

Although such assistive technology can reduce some short-term memory cost, the linear nature of braille system across literary and mathematics symbols makes it hard for blind people to get a high level picture of complex equations which would typically be represented in a two-dimentional visual form for sighted people (Beck-Winchatz & Riccobono, 2008; Seo & McCurry, 2019).
