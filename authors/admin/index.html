JooYoung Seo

Ph.D. Candidate (ABD) in Learning, Design, and Technology

The Pennsylvania University

JooYoung Seo is a Ph.D. candidate (ABD) in the Learning, Design, and Technology program at the Penn State University, and internationally certified accessibility professional (CPACC).

He also works as an instructional developer (graduate assistant) at the TLT (Teaching and Learning with Technology) IT Accessibility Team at Penn State. As an accessibility/assistive? technology expert, he has been training HTML5 and ARIA (the Accessible Rich Internet Applications) for other colleagues at the TLT studio since March 2015. His another important role in the office is to test the web accessibility standards of various Penn State web sites, other educational institutes, learning management systems, and eBook products, and provide professional advice for better screen reader access based on WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) 2.0.

As a software engineer, he has also developed and published some open-source R statistical computing packages to the peer-reviewed Comprehensive R Archive Network (CRAN), such as “ezpickr”, “mboxr”, “youtubecaption”, etc. Furthermore, he is the developer of “tactileR” R package, a statistical software function that converts visual data into tactile graphs in conjunction with swell touch paper.

He is the first-ever blind grantee awarded the Global Korea Scholarship in Future Interdisplinary Study from the Korean government for his graduate research (2014-2016), and has received the Delta Gamma Golden Anchor Award from the Penn State University for his great academic achievement (2015-2016), the Outstanding Korean Blind Leader Scholarship (2014-2016) from the Siloam Foundation in Korea, and the Yoon Family’s Award (2015) from the Three Yoons Foundation in Korea.

He is interested in how to actively engage learners with disabilities in learning ecology, including online learning, mobile learning, maker spaces, and game-based learning. Recently, he has focused his attention on improving accessibility for visually impaired learners in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) fields by employing assistive technologies.
